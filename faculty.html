INDUSTRIAL TECHNOLOGY

Department of Industrial Technology

The Department of Industrial Technology is committed to train students to become skilled workers, knowledgeable and competent industrial technicians, supervisors, and managers who will contribute immensely to the attainment of socio-economic stability of the region and the whole nation. The Department focuses on technical-vocational education with specializations in civil and drafting technology, computer technology, electrical technology, electronics technology, food preparation and service management, and garments technology.

The Commission on Higher Education awarded the renewal of the Certificate of Program Compliance to the Department to offer the Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology through COPC No. 31, series of 2021. Currently, the program is awarded Level III Re-accredited status by the Accrediting Agency of Chartered Colleges and Universities in the Philippines (AACCUP), Inc.

The 2020 curriculum of the BSInTech was approved by the Board of Regents through the BOR Resolution No. 002-REF, series of 2020. To ensure that the competencies learned from each field of specialization are industry-relevant, the major courses are designed following the Training Regulations of TESDA under the specific competencies.

Automotive Technology

Department of Automotive Technology

The Bachelor in Automotive Technology (BAT) started as one of the specialization courses of the BS in Industrial Technology program of the College of Industrial Technology in 1976. It was re-aligned as an independent program and department in 2008 with Board Resolution No. 953, series of 2008.

The Commission on Higher Education awarded the renewal of the Certificate of Program Compliance to the Department to offer the Bachelor in Automotive Technology under COPC No. 12, series of 2021, with an approved curriculum under BOR Resolution No. 002-REF, series of 2020. Currently, the program is at Level III Phase I accreditation status by the Accrediting Agency of Chartered Colleges and Universities in the Philippines (AACCUP), Inc.

The Department hosts the Toyota Technology Center (TTC), which is one among 18 established in nominated partner schools in the country under the Toyota Automotive Education Program (TAEP) of the Toyota Motor Philippines Foundation (TMPF) Inc. The TTC envisions to become the regional skills development center in automotive servicing.
